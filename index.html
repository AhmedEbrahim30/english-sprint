"What's up?" / "What's going on?"

فرصة سعيدة إني قابلتك أخيراً — لمن كنت بتكلمه online
📚 Vocab — Work 2 items
Deadline /ˈdedlaɪn/
الموعد النهائي
"The project deadline is end of next month."
Stakeholder /ˈsteɪkˌhoʊldər/
صاحب مصلحة، طرف معني
"We need to align all stakeholders before the launch."
🎯 Grammar — Present Simple
Use: للحقائق، العادات، الروتين
Form: Subject + base verb (+s for he/she/it)
  1. "I manage the network team."
  2. "She doesn't usually attend the morning standup."
  3. "Does the new system support that?"
🎤 Conversation
First day a new IT colleague joins the project team.
Sarah: Hi! You must be the new team member. I'm Sarah, I just joined IT.
You: Yes, nice to finally meet you. I've heard a lot about you.
Sarah: Hopefully good things! How's it going so far?
You: Busy, but good. We're in the middle of a big handover.
Sarah: Right — the system migration? I heard about it.
You: Yeah, 100+ devices across all our locations. The deadline is tight.
Sarah: Who are the main stakeholders?
You: The IT director, security ops, and a couple of contractor leads. I'll introduce you tomorrow.
Sarah: Perfect. Looking forward to it.
🔁 اقراها مرتين بصوت عالي. ركز على "I've heard" و "We're in"

"Let's loop in [name] on this." — خلينا نضم [اسم] للموضوع. مفيدة جداً في الإيميلات والاجتماعات.

📚 Vocab — Work 3 items
Follow up /ˈfɒloʊ ʌp/
يتابع
"I'll follow up with the vendor tomorrow."
Bandwidth /ˈbændwɪdθ/
الوقت/الطاقة المتاحة — مش بس سعة الشبكة

Bottleneck /ˈbɒtlnek/
عنق الزجاجة، نقطة تعطل
"Procurement is the bottleneck in the rollout."
🎯 Grammar — Present Continuous
Use: حاجة بتحصل دلوقتي، أو خطة مؤقتة
Form: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
  1. "I'm working on the new switch config right now."
  2. "The contractor team isn't responding to our tickets."
  3. "Are you joining the system review call at 3?"
⚡ الفرق: I work in networks = وظيفتي · I'm working on a migration = دلوقتي/مؤقت
🎤 Conversation
Morning team standup. The manager goes around the team.
Manager: Morning. Where are we on the system migration?
You: About 70% done. The remaining zones should be finished by Thursday.
Manager: Any blockers?
You: The approval process is a bottleneck. Procurement is taking longer than expected.
Manager: Let me circle back to you on that — I'll follow up with them today.
You: Appreciate it. Also, I don't have the bandwidth for the new switch rollout this week.
Manager: Noted. We'll push that to next sprint.
Sarah: Quick question — can someone walk me through the network setup?
You: I'm on it. Let's sync after this.
💡 Pro Phrase for Your Work
"Let's table this for now." — خلينا نأجل ده دلوقتي. مفيدة لما النقاش بياخد وقت ومش وقته.

📚 Vocab — Relationships 2 items

🎯 Grammar — Past Simple
Use: حاجة خلصت في وقت معين
Form: verb-ed (regular) / special form (irregular)
  1. "I worked at a telecom company for five years."
  2. "She didn't come to the party last night."
  3. "Did you see the email yesterday?"
⚠️ شاذة لازم تحفظها: go→went, see→saw, take→took, have→had, get→got, make→made
🎤 Conversation
Phone call with an old friend after months of silence.
Omar: Hey! It's been forever. How are you, man?
You: Omar! I know — work has been crazy. How are you doing?
Omar: No worries. I get it. I wanted to reach out for a while.
You: Same here. We should catch up properly. Coffee this weekend?
Omar: Definitely. Did you hear about Khaled? He moved to Dubai last month.
You: Really? I had no idea. Good for him.
Omar: Yeah, he started a new job. Said the move was exhausting but worth it.
You: I see what you mean. I felt the same when I changed companies.
Omar: Saturday at 4? The usual place?
You: Perfect. I really appreciate you reaching out, Omar.
💡 Pro Phrase
"Long time no see / talk." — من زمان مشفناكش — تعبير شعبي شائع جداً.

📚 Vocab — Relationships 3 items
Get along (with)

"I really get along with my team."
Fall out (with)
يتخانق ويبعد
"They fell out over a misunderstanding."
Look out for
يخلي باله من حد، يحميه
"He always looks out for his younger brother."
🎯 Grammar — Future (will vs going to)
will — قرار اللحظة، توقع، وعد
  1. "I'll call you later." — on-the-spot decision
  2. "It will rain tomorrow, I think." — prediction
  3. "I promise I won't forget." — promise
going to — خطة موجودة قبل ما تتكلم
  1. "I'm going to start gym next week." — pre-planned
  2. "We're going to launch the system in March."
  3. "Look at the sky — it's going to rain." — visible evidence
🎤 Conversation
Coworker felt cut off in yesterday's meeting. She brings it up.
Layla: Hey, do you have a minute?
You: Sure, what's up?
Layla: About the meeting yesterday — when you cut me off, it bothered me.
You: Oh… I didn't mean it that way. I thought you'd finished. I'm really sorry.
Layla: I appreciate you saying that. That makes sense actually.
You: I totally get it. I'll be more careful next time.
Layla: Thanks. We've always gotten along well — I didn't want it to become a thing.
You: I'm glad you said something. I'd rather hear it directly.
Layla: Coffee later? My treat.
You: I'm going to take you up on that.
💡 Pro Phrase
"My bad." — غلطة مني — قصيرة لما تكون أنت غلطان وعايز تعترف بسرعة.

Jet lag /ˈdʒet læɡ/

🎯 Grammar — Modal Verbs
could — احتمال، طلب مؤدب، قدرة قديمة
  1. "Could you help me?" — polite request
  2. "It could rain later." — possibility
  3. "I could speak French when I was younger." — past ability
should = نصيحة/توقع · might = احتمال أقل تأكيداً
🎤 Conversation
Business trip overseas. Wrong gate at the airport.
You: Excuse me, could you help me out? I think I'm at the wrong gate.
Agent: Sure, can I see your boarding pass?
You: Here you go.
Agent: Your gate changed — it's now Gate 32, not 22. You should hurry, they might start boarding soon.
You: How do I get there fastest?
Agent: Take the escalator down, then walk straight for five minutes. You can't miss it.
You: Thanks. By the way, is there a long layover?
Agent: Two hours in Doha. Not bad.
You: Could be worse. Thanks again — I should run!
💡 Pro Phrase
"I'd appreciate it if you could…" — هكون ممنون لو ممكن… أكثر تعبير مؤدب لطلب.

Translate to English: ينفع تساعدني أوصل للمحطة؟

🎯 Grammar — Mixing Tenses
النهاردة، نخلط كل القواعد في جمل واحدة:
  1. "I traveled to Spain last summer, and I'm planning to go back next year."
  2. "She works in marketing, but she's currently leading a special project."
  3. "You should book early — flights are going to get expensive."
🎤 Conversation
After a long flight, you arrive at the hotel. The room isn't what you booked.
Receptionist: Good evening. Checking in?
You: Yes, reservation under "Ebrahim." King room, three nights.
Receptionist: Let me see… I'm showing a twin room.
You: There's been a misunderstanding. I booked a king. I have the confirmation.
Receptionist: I'm so sorry. Let me check.
You: I'd like to speak to the manager if possible.
Receptionist: Actually — we have a king suite available. I can upgrade you at no extra cost.
You: That works. Thank you.
Receptionist: Any restaurant recommendations you'd like?
You: Yes — what do you recommend? Somewhere off the beaten path.
Receptionist: Try "La Cantina" two blocks down. Locals only.
💡 Pro Phrase
"Is there any way you could…?" — في أي طريقة ممكن…؟ مؤدبة جداً للمواقف الصعبة.
